    • “San Isidro Labrador Parish Church”

      "Lazi Church"
      Year Built: 1884
      Located: Lazi, Siquijor
      Architectural Styles: Neoclassical Architecture

    Lazi (formerly Tigbawan) broke away from Siquijor on August 8, 1857, to become its own parish. The bell tower and the existing stone church were built by Filipino artisans in 1884 and 1885, respectively. The monastery was made from coral stones and oak between 1887 and 1891. The cathedral, which is renowned for its sizable convent, was named a National Cultural Treasure by the Philippine National Museum. The government declared its intention to add Lazi Church to the World Heritage List in 2014. The Lazi Church also features two pulpits, which offer another fascinating detail that I have only ever noticed at this site and balance out the interior's symmetry. The Lazi Convent is one of the largest convents in the Philippines. Its construction started in 1887, and in 1891 it was blessed.
