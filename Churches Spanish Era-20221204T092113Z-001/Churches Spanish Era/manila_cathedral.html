    • “Manila Cathedral”

      "Cathedral de Manila"
      Year Built: 1571
      Located: Plaza de Roma, Intramuros
      Architectural Styles: Neo Romanesque

    The cathedral and basilica of Manila, known in Spanish as the Cathedral de Manila, are situated in Intramuros, the old walled city that is now a part of the contemporary metropolis of Manila, Philippines. A secular priest named Fray Juan de Vivero first erected the cathedral as the "church of Manila" in 1571 after sailing into Manila Bay in 1566. Alonso de Montfar, the Archbishop of Mexico, dispatched De Vivero, the galleon of San Geronimo's chaplain, to establish Christianity as the spiritual and religious leadership in the newly acquired Philippines. Miguel López de Legazpi, a Spanish conquistador, chose the site for the chapel and gave it the patronage of Santa Potenciana. Fray Juan de Villanueva served as the church's first parish pastor. A new building made of nipa, wood, and bamboo was built in 1581 by Domingo de Salazar, the first bishop of Manila, after the church was elevated to a cathedral in 1579 (coinciding with the canonical erection of the Diocese of Manila).
